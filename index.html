Coffee's origins can be found in Ethiopia in the ninth century, when a goat herder noticed the beverage's stimulating properties. It became a beloved beverage all over the world as it expanded throughout the Arab world and beyond.

COFFEE

"You can't buy happiness, but you can buy coffee, and that's pretty close."

Coffee beans, or the seeds of the Coffea plant, are roasted and used to make the popular beverage coffee. Coffea arabica and Coffea robusta are the two most widely utilized and significant species in terms of commerce. Coffee is usually made by grinding the roasted beans and then brewing them in hot water to release their scents and flavors. People from all across the world have long liked coffee. Caffeine, a naturally occurring stimulant, can help boost attention and lessen weariness. Numerous variables, including the kind of beans used, the degree of roasting, and the brewing technique, can affect how coffee tastes.
